HISTORY

Here we can see the years and when the first vape was invented.

First E-Cig

1963

a man named Herbert Gilbert patented a device that produced a vapor from tobacco by heat rather than combustion. Despite several tobacco companies taking an interest in the device, it was never commercialized.

Hon Lik invents the first e-cigarette

2003

Hon Lik's e-cigarette consisted of a battery, an atomizer, and a cartridge containing liquid nicotine, which was vaporized by the atomizer and inhaled by the user. The e-cigarette was designed to mimic the experience of smoking a traditional cigarette, but without the harmful tar and other toxic chemicals that are produced by burning tobacco.

E-cigarettes are introduced to the market

2004

Hon Lik's e-cigarette was introduced to the market in 2004, and it quickly gained popularity as a safer alternative to smoking. The e-cigarette was particularly appealing to smokers who were trying to quit, as it provided them with a way to satisfy their nicotine cravings without exposing themselves to the harmful chemicals found in traditional cigarettes.

E-cigarettes become widely available

2007

By 2007, e-cigarettes had become widely available in many countries around the world, and their popularity continued to grow. Many smokers saw e-cigarettes as a way to reduce their risk of tobacco-related diseases, and the e-cigarette industry began to expand rapidly.

The first e-cigarette brands are established

2009

The e-cigarette market continues to grow

2013

By 2013, the e-cigarette market had grown significantly, with sales reaching an estimated $1 billion worldwide. E-cigarettes had become a popular choice for smokers looking to quit, and they were also being used recreationally by many people who had never smoked before.

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) regulates e-cigarettes

2018

In 2018, the FDA announced that it would begin regulating e-cigarettes in the United States. The agency imposed strict rules on the production, marketing, and sale of e-cigarettes, including a ban on the sale of e-cigarettes to minors.

The global e-cigarette market continues to grow

2020

Despite regulatory challenges and controversy surrounding the safety of e-cigarettes, the global e-cigarette market continues to grow. In 2020, the market was valued at an estimated $16 billion, and it is expected to continue to expand in the coming years. In conclusion, the evolution of vaping and the development of the first e-cigarette have had a significant impact on the way people consume nicotine. While there are still debates about the safety of e-cigarettes, they have proven to be a popular and effective alternative to traditional cigarettes for many people looking to quit smoking.
